## About the Collection

The life of Mary Sherwood was a unique one in her era. Partway through her life she discovered is art of anthropomorphic taxidermy, or taxidermy animals dressed up or put in scenes. While an interest in death was not uncommon in the Victorian era, her particular manifestation of this interest differed from most other. As did her sexuality. Persephone Collins was Mary’s closest and dearest friends, and many scholars believed it was something more. They spent much time writing letters to one another and even more in each other’s company. At one point in time, Persephone moved in with Mary and they stayed together for the rest of their lives.
